CHAPTER XXII. DISTORTION OF WAVE-SHAPE AND ITS CAUSES. 233. In the preceding chapters we have considered the alternating currents and alternating E.M.Fs. as sine waves or as replaced by their equivalent sine waves. While this is sufficiently exact in most cases, under certain circumstances the deviation of the wave from sine shape becomes of importance, and with certain distortions it may not be possible to replace the distorted wave by an equivalent sine wave, since the angle of phase displacement of the equivalent sine wave becomes indefinite.
