LECTURE IX. MEASUREMENT OF LIGHT AND RADIATION. 74. Since radiation is energy, it can be measured as such by converting the energy of radiation into some other form of energy, as, for instance, into heat, and measuring the latter. Thus a beam of radiation may be measured by having it impinge on one contact of a thermo-couple, of which the other contact is maintained at constant temperature. A galvanom- eter in the circuit of this thermo-couple thus measures the voltage produced by the difference of temperature of the two contacts of the thermo-couple, and in this manner the temper- ature rise produced by the energy of the incident beam of radia- tion is observed.
