INTRODUCTION 1. By the direction of the energy transmitted, electric machines have been divided into generators and motors. By the character of the electric power they have been distinguished as direct- current and as alternating-current apparatus. With the advance of electrical engineering, however, these subdivisions have become unsatisfactory and insufficient. The division into generators and motors is not based on any characteristic feature of the apparatus, and is thus not rational. Practically any electric generator can be used as motor, and conversely, and frequently one and the same machine is used for either purpose.
